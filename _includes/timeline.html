Timeline

  • Take a break. read and research on UX/UI.

    After leaving my previous company, I spent a few months traveling and volunteering. While taking the time to cross off things on my bucket list, I did reading and research on UX and UI design, and started by figuring out my strength and weakness.

  • Make S.M.A.R.T goals: my design study plan

    With advice from Karen, who taught herself design in 6 months and landed a job at a startup, I started to execute my own study plan.

    This post by a product designer at Pinterest helped me fine tune my study on UX/UI.

  • Start with the foundation: Sketching and Graphic Design

    • Draw 1 hour everyday to practice my sketching.
    • Take the course on Coursera to build a foundation on color, typography and designing by grid. Read smash magazine to keep up with the latest trend and techniques.

  • Learn and Practise: UX and UI

    • Take the course on Coursera to learn methodology on user experience research and prototyping.
    • Learn the basic and advanced techniques in Interaction Design on Lynda.com to make effective, engaging and interactive design.
    • Re-designing existing websites and mobile apps. Practice is the best way to learn design.
